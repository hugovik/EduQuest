# User Personas

---

# Purpose

This document defines the primary users of EduQuest. Understanding each user type ensures that product decisions support real needs rather than assumptions.

EduQuest is designed for an ecosystem of learners, families, and educators. Every feature should provide value to at least one persona without negatively impacting the experience of the others.

---

# Primary Persona — The Explorer

## Overview

The Explorer is the child using EduQuest.

Everything in the application is ultimately designed to create an engaging, educational, and emotionally rewarding experience for this player.

---

## Profile

Age

6–12 years

Environment

- Home
- School
- Library
- After-school programs

Devices

- Tablet
- Laptop
- Desktop
- Chromebook

---

## Goals

The Explorer wants to:

- Have fun
- Discover new places
- Complete adventures
- Grow their Tree
- Unlock companions
- Collect achievements
- Solve mysteries
- Feel successful

Learning is the tool that allows these goals to be achieved.

---

## Motivations

The Explorer enjoys:

- Progress
- Stories
- Exploration
- Characters
- Rewards
- Personal ownership
- Collecting
- Discovery

---

## Frustrations

Children lose interest when:

- Lessons repeat unnecessarily
- Progress feels invisible
- Interfaces are confusing
- Failure is overly emphasized
- Rewards feel meaningless
- Sessions become too long

---

## Design Considerations

The Explorer requires:

- Simple navigation
- Clear objectives
- Immediate feedback
- Positive reinforcement
- Visible progression
- Consistent interface
- Encouraging language

---

# Secondary Persona — The Parent

## Overview

Parents support the child's learning journey.

Their primary concern is educational value rather than gameplay.

Parents should feel informed without needing to supervise every activity.

---

## Goals

Parents want to:

- Encourage learning
- Monitor progress
- Celebrate achievements
- Identify difficulties
- Build positive habits
- Reduce resistance to learning

---

## Motivations

Parents value:

- Educational quality
- Safety
- Privacy
- Progress reports
- Clear recommendations
- Healthy screen time

---

## Frustrations

Parents dislike:

- Excessive advertising
- Hidden purchases
- Poor educational value
- Confusing reports
- Overly addictive mechanics
- Lack of transparency

---

## Design Considerations

Parents require:

- Clear dashboards
- Weekly summaries
- Achievement notifications
- Learning insights
- Privacy controls
- Account management

---

# Third Persona — The Teacher

## Overview

Teachers integrate EduQuest into classroom activities.

The platform should reduce administrative effort while increasing student engagement.

---

## Goals

Teachers want to:

- Assign learning activities
- Monitor classroom progress
- Support struggling students
- Recognize achievements
- Encourage participation

---

## Motivations

Teachers value:

- Curriculum alignment
- Time savings
- Student engagement
- Reliable reporting
- Easy classroom management

---

## Frustrations

Teachers avoid systems that:

- Require extensive setup
- Generate unnecessary administration
- Produce unreliable data
- Distract students
- Lack educational credibility

---

## Design Considerations

Teachers require:

- Classroom dashboard
- Assignment management
- Progress reports
- Achievement overview
- Exportable reports

---

# Future Persona — Educational Content Creator

Content creators build new educational adventures.

Responsibilities include:

- Writing quests
- Creating stories
- Defining rewards
- Building lesson sequences
- Maintaining educational standards

Future versions may include dedicated authoring tools.

---

# Persona Relationships

```
Parent
    │
    ▼

Child (Explorer)

    ▲
    │

Teacher
```

The child remains at the center of every interaction.

Parents and teachers support the child's learning journey.

Neither should reduce the child's sense of ownership over their world.

---

# Design Priorities

When priorities conflict:

1. Child Learning
2. Child Experience
3. Parent Insight
4. Teacher Productivity
5. Administrative Efficiency

The child's educational experience always comes first.

---

# Success Indicators

The Explorer should:

- Want to return tomorrow.
- Feel proud of their progress.
- Understand what to do next.
- Enjoy discovering new content.

Parents should:

- Trust the platform.
- See measurable progress.
- Feel informed without micromanaging.

Teachers should:

- Save time.
- Improve classroom engagement.
- Gain useful educational insights.
