# Worlds

---

# Purpose

This document defines the world structure of EduQuest.

The educational experience is organized into themed worlds rather than traditional subjects. Each world represents a different area of learning while contributing to one unified progression system.

Worlds are not merely collections of lessons.

They are places that players explore, restore, and eventually master.

---

# Vision

The EduQuest universe is a living world that has slowly faded after the disappearance of the Ancient Keepers of Knowledge.

Knowledge itself has become fragmented.

Professor Owl discovers that only curious young explorers can restore the world's forgotten wisdom.

Every lesson completed by the player restores another piece of the world.

Learning literally rebuilds civilization.

---

# World Structure

The world is divided into educational realms.

```
                     EduQuest World

                           │

    ┌──────────────┬──────────────┬──────────────┐
    │              │              │
Reading Forest  Math Mountain  Writing Castle
    │              │              │
Science Lab    Music Garden   History Kingdom
    │
Coding Academy
```

Each world focuses on a specific educational discipline while sharing common gameplay systems.

---

# Shared World Principles

Every world should include:

- unique visual identity
- memorable characters
- educational objectives
- progressive difficulty
- hidden discoveries
- collectibles
- achievements
- story progression

Players should immediately recognize which world they are visiting.

# World Progression

Every world progresses through similar stages.

```
Arrival

↓

Discovery

↓

Exploration

↓

Challenges

↓

Restoration

↓

Mastery

↓

Hidden Secrets

↓

World Complete
```

Completion unlocks new narrative chapters and rewards.

---

# Educational Progression

Worlds become progressively more challenging.

Difficulty increases through:

- educational complexity
- larger adventures
- integrated problem solving
- multi-step quests

Learning should remain accessible while continuously encouraging growth.

---

# Cross-World Integration

Although each world teaches different skills, they are interconnected.

Examples:

- Reading clues unlock math puzzles.
- Writing spells activate scientific experiments.
- Mathematical calculations reveal historical timelines.
- Science discoveries inspire creative writing.

This encourages interdisciplinary learning.

- Ocean Kingdom
- Space Observatory

- Language Islands
- Robotics Factory

The EduQuest universe should continue growing for many years.

---

# Success Criteria

A world succeeds when players:

- recognize it instantly
- understand its educational purpose
- enjoy exploring it
- remember its characters
- feel motivated to return

Every world should become a place children want to visit—not simply another collection of lessons.
