# Progression System

---

# Purpose

This document defines how players progress through EduQuest over time.

Progression is the foundation of long-term engagement. Every completed learning activity contributes to the player's growth, making educational achievements meaningful and visible.

Unlike traditional educational software, EduQuest measures progress not only through academic performance but also through the evolution of the player's world.

---

# Design Goals

The progression system is designed to:

- Reward consistent learning.
- Encourage curiosity.
- Celebrate effort.
- Create long-term motivation.
- Provide visible progress.
- Unlock meaningful content.
- Avoid repetitive grinding.

Progress should always feel earned.

---

# Types of Progression

EduQuest includes multiple progression layers.

## Immediate Progression

Occurs within seconds.

Examples:

- Correct answer
- XP gained
- Positive animation
- Companion reaction

Immediate progression provides instant satisfaction.

---

## Session Progression

Occurs during a single learning session.

Examples:

- Quest completion
- Story advancement
- Temporary rewards
- Bonus discoveries

Each session should feel meaningful.

---

## Daily Progression

Occurs across multiple activities completed in one day.

Examples:

- Daily Quest
- Reading Streak
- Daily XP Goal
- Companion Interaction

Daily progression encourages healthy learning habits.

---

## Long-Term Progression

Occurs over weeks and months.

Examples:

- Level increases
- Tree Growth
- New Worlds
- Companion evolution
- Story Chapters
- Achievement collection

Long-term progression gives players reasons to continue learning.

---

# Player Level

Every player has a level.

Levels represent overall educational progress.

Levels are earned only through XP gained from meaningful educational activities.

Levels cannot be purchased.

Levels cannot be skipped.

---

## Level Formula

Initial implementation:

```
Level 1

0 XP
```

```
Level 2

100 XP
```

```
Level 3

250 XP
```

```
Level 4

```

Progressive scaling prevents rapid completion while maintaining motivation.

Future versions may introduce adaptive progression curves.

---

# Experience Points (XP)

XP is awarded for educational achievements.

Examples:

- Completing quests
- Reading stories
- Solving math challenges
- Daily login
- Bonus exploration
- Helping companions

XP should always reflect meaningful learning.

---

Tree stages include:

```
Seed

↓

Seedling

↓

Sapling

↓

↓

Growing Tree

↓

Flourishing Tree

↓

Ancient Tree

↓

Tree of Wisdom
```

Tree Growth becomes the primary visual representation of progress.

---

# World Progression

Learning unlocks new locations.

Players gradually restore and explore:

- Reading Forest
- Math Mountain
- Writing Castle
- Science Laboratory
- Music Garden
- History Kingdom

Each world introduces new educational experiences.

---

# Story Progression

The main story advances naturally through learning.

Players uncover:

- Forgotten civilizations
- Ancient libraries
- Magical creatures
- Hidden villages
- Lost knowledge

Story progression never replaces learning.

Learning unlocks the story.

---

# Companion Progression

Companions develop alongside the player.

Growth may include:

- New dialogue
- Personality development
- Visual changes
- Helpful abilities
- New interactions

Companions should feel like long-term friends rather than temporary rewards.

---

# Achievement Progression

Achievements celebrate important milestones.

Categories include:

Learning

Exploration

Consistency

Creativity

Kindness

Problem Solving

World Discovery

Secret Achievements

Achievements provide recognition without becoming mandatory objectives.

---

# Collection Progression

Players gradually collect:

- Books
- Relics
- Story Pages
- Decorations
- Companion Items
- Seasonal Objects

Collections create additional long-term goals.

---

---

# Daily Streaks

Consistency is rewarded.

Examples:

3 Days

7 Days

14 Days

30 Days

365 Days

Missing a day should not erase long-term progress.

Instead, the system should encourage returning without punishment.

---

# Mastery Progression

EduQuest values understanding over speed.

Before advancing significantly, players should demonstrate mastery.

Mastery includes:

- Accuracy
- Confidence
- Consistency
- Retention

Adaptive review ensures long-term learning.

---

# Progress Dashboard

Players can review:

- Current Level
- Total XP
- Tree Stage
- Worlds Unlocked
- Quests Completed
- Books Read
- Achievements
- Companion Status
- Learning Streak

Progress should be simple to understand.

---

# Parent Progress

Parents view:

- Weekly Growth
- Educational Strengths
- Areas for Improvement
- Learning Habits
- Milestones

Reports emphasize growth rather than comparison.

---

# Teacher Progress

Teachers review:

- Classroom Completion
- Student Growth
- Assignment Status
- Skill Development
- Achievement Overview

Progress information supports classroom instruction.

---

# Future Progression

Future versions may introduce:

- Skill Trees
- Companion Skill Paths
- Seasonal Progress
- Community Restoration Projects
- Personal Learning Goals
- AI-Generated Learning Paths

Each new progression system must reinforce education rather than distract from it.

---

# Guiding Principles

Progression should always be:

- Visible
- Meaningful
- Earned
- Educational
- Encouraging
- Long-term
- Scalable

Progress should never become repetitive or manipulative.

---

# Progression Statement

EduQuest transforms learning into visible growth.

Every page read, every puzzle solved, every story completed, and every challenge overcome contributes to a world that becomes richer because the player chose to learn.

Progress is not measured only by numbers.

It is measured by the world the child creates through knowledge.
