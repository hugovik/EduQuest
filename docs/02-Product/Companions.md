# Companions

---

# Purpose

Companions are the player's closest friends throughout their EduQuest journey.

Unlike traditional game companions that primarily assist in combat, EduQuest companions exist to encourage curiosity, celebrate learning, provide emotional support, and make the world feel alive.

Companions are never replacements for teachers or parents.

They are loyal friends who grow alongside the player.

---

# Vision

Every child should feel emotionally connected to their companion.

The companion should remember previous adventures, react to achievements, celebrate milestones, and occasionally surprise the player with small moments of delight.

Years later, players should remember their companion as one of the defining parts of EduQuest.

---

# Design Principles

Companions should always be:

- encouraging
- curious
- playful
- supportive
- emotionally expressive
- educational

Companions should never become annoying, repetitive, or distracting.

---

# Companion Roles

Companions perform several important functions.

## Emotional Support

Celebrate success.

Encourage persistence.

Reduce frustration.

Create positive emotional memories.

---

## Gameplay Support

Companions may:

- introduce quests
- provide hints
- celebrate achievements
- discover secrets
- unlock optional content
- react to world events

They never complete learning activities for the player.

---

## Storytelling

Companions participate in the main story.

They:

- ask questions
- notice hidden clues
- remember previous adventures
- develop relationships with world characters

This strengthens the narrative.

---

# Starting Companion

## Spark Dragon

Spark is every player's first companion.

Spark is a young dragon who feeds on curiosity rather than fire.

Knowledge is Spark's favorite treasure.

Whenever the player learns something new, Spark becomes stronger.

---

## Personality

Spark is:

- energetic
- optimistic
- adventurous
- loyal
- occasionally clumsy
- endlessly curious

Spark often notices interesting things before the player does.

---

# Companion Growth

Companions evolve over time.

Growth depends upon:

- learning
- exploration
- consistency
- friendship

Growth never depends on purchases.

---

---

# Friendship Level

Each companion has a friendship level.

Friendship increases through:

- daily interaction
- completed quests
- exploration
- story progression

Friendship unlocks:

- conversations
- memories
- gifts
- special events

---

# Companion Abilities

Companions unlock useful educational abilities.

Examples:

Spark

- notices hidden clues

Luna Fox

- discovers secret paths

Atlas Bear

- remembers historical facts

Pixel Penguin

- explains programming concepts

Willow Turtle

- provides calming encouragement

Abilities should enhance exploration without reducing educational challenge.

---

# Companion Home

Every companion has a place within the Tree House.

Examples:

Spark

- sleeps near the fireplace

Luna

- enjoys the garden

Pixel Penguin

- builds gadgets in the workshop

Children should always know where to find their companions.

---

# Daily Interactions

Companions should greet players differently each day.

Examples:

"Good morning! Professor Owl left us another adventure!"

"I found something interesting while you were away."

"The Tree looks bigger today!"

"I wonder what's inside the Old Library..."

Dialogue should feel natural rather than repetitive.

---

# Companion Requests

Occasionally companions ask for help.

Examples:

"Can we read another story today?"

"I wonder how bridges are built."

"I heard strange music coming from the forest."

These requests gently encourage learning activities.

---

# Companion Memories

Companions remember important milestones.

Examples:

- first completed quest
- first book read
- first achievement
- first world completed
- birthday celebrations

Future conversations reference these memories.

This creates continuity.

---

# Companion Gifts

Occasionally companions reward the player.

Examples:

- feathers
- flowers
- story pages
- decorations
- collectible artifacts

Gifts are symbolic rather than powerful.

---

# Emotional States

Companions express emotions through animation.

Examples:

Happy

Excited

Curious

Sleepy

Proud

Thoughtful

Concerned

These emotions should always remain positive and comforting.

---

# Companion Conversations

Conversations should be:

- short
- meaningful
- optional
- age appropriate

Examples include:

- observations
- jokes
- questions
- encouragement
- interesting facts

Dialogue should avoid unnecessary repetition.

---

# Seasonal Activities

Companions celebrate seasons.

Examples:

Spring

Collect flowers.

Summer

Play outside.

Autumn

Gather colorful leaves.

Winter

Build snow dragons.

Holiday events should strengthen emotional attachment.

# Companion Selection

Future versions may allow players to choose an active companion.

Other companions remain in the Tree House.

This encourages collection without replacing previous friendships.

---

# Technical Design

Each companion consists of:

```
Name

Species

Personality

Animations

Dialogue Library

Abilities

Friendship Level

Growth Stage

Story Progress

Seasonal Variants
```

The architecture should allow unlimited future companions.

---

# Success Criteria

Companions succeed when children:

- look forward to seeing them
- remember their personalities
- enjoy conversations
- celebrate milestones together
- feel emotionally connected

Children should never think of companions as interface elements.

They should feel like friends.

---

# Vision Statement

Companions transform EduQuest from educational software into a shared adventure.

Knowledge may restore the world.

But friendship makes the journey unforgettable.
